You create a group, invite the people you want, upload your tracks, and everyone in the group can listen in the browser.

## What is Snipit?

Snipit gives you a personal music locker that you share with a circle of people you choose. There are no public profiles, no discovery feeds, and no social graph — just private groups where members upload and listen to tracks together.

Each group has its own track list and member roster. You control who joins by sharing an invite link. Members can upload MP3 or MP4 audio files up to 50 MB, and everyone in the group can stream those tracks directly in the browser without downloading anything.

## Tech stack

Snipit is built with a modern, self-hostable stack:

| Layer | Technology |
|---|---|
| Framework | [Next.js](https://nextjs.org/) 14 (App Router) |
| Authentication | [Supabase Auth](https://supabase.com/docs/guides/auth) via Google OAuth |
| Database | [Supabase](https://supabase.com/) (PostgreSQL) |
| File storage | [Supabase Storage](https://supabase.com/docs/guides/storage) (the `mp4` bucket) |
| Styling | [Tailwind CSS](https://tailwindcss.com/) |
| UI components | [Radix UI](https://www.radix-ui.com/) primitives |
| State management | [Zustand](https://zustand-demo.pmnd.rs/) |
| Data fetching | [SWR](https://swr.vercel.app/) |

<Note>
  Snipit uses Google OAuth for authentication. Users sign in with their Google account — there is no email/password registration flow.
</Note>

## Who is Snipit for?

Snipit is for small, trusted groups of people who want to share audio files privately. Common use cases include:

- **Friend groups** sharing original music, demos, or recordings with each other
- **Collaborators** exchanging work-in-progress tracks without uploading to a public platform
- **Music enthusiasts** curating a shared collection of local files with a small circle

Snipit is not a public streaming service. There is no discovery, no public profiles, and no way for strangers to access your groups. Every group is invite-only.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in with Google">
    Go to `/login` and click **Continue with Google**. Snipit uses Supabase Auth to handle authentication — no password required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a group">
    From your dashboard, click **Create Group**. Give it a name and an optional description. The group appears in your dashboard immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload a track">
    Open the group and click **Upload Track**. Select an MP3 or MP4 file (up to 50 MB), enter the track title and artist name, and submit. The file is stored in Supabase Storage and the metadata is saved to Supabase.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite members">
    Click **Invite Members** inside the group to generate an invite link. Share the link with anyone you want to add. When they open the link, they are automatically added to the group after signing in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Listen together">
    Every member of the group can see the shared tracks and play them in the browser. Your dashboard shows recently uploaded tracks across all your groups.
  </Step>
</Steps>
